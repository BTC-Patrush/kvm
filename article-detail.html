Finding Peace in the Chaos of Kathmandu

Pastor John Doe Dec 20, 2025 â€¢ 5 min read

Kathmandu is a city of senses. The sound of horns, the smell of incense and exhaust, the vibrant colors of prayer flags fluttering against grey buildings. It is beautiful, energetic, and often, overwhelming. In the midst of this constant motion, how do we cultivate a "quiet heart" as scripture commands?

The Noise Within

Often, the noise outside is just a reflection of the noise inside. We worry about tomorrow, we replay yesterday, and we miss the presence of God in the present moment. Psalm 46:10 says, "Be still, and know that I am God." The Hebrew word for "be still" implies ceasing from striving, letting go, relaxing our grip.

Practical Steps for stillness

  • Start your day with silence: Before checking your phone, spend 5 minutes in silence.
  • Create a sacred space: Find a corner in your home that is dedicated to prayer.
  • Scripture meditation: Instead of reading large chunks, meditate on a single verse throughout the day.
"Peace is not the absence of trouble, but the presence of Christ."

As we navigate the busy streets of our city this week, let us carry a portable sanctuary within us. When the traffic stops, instead of frustration, choose prayer. When the noise rises, let your praise rise higher. God is not distant from the chaos; He is the peace within it.
